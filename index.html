Complete Process · 6 Steps · First Campaign
Meta Ads Workflow
Research → Creative → Campaign Setup → Launch → Optimize
1
🔵
Research Competitors
Facebook Ad Library — facebook.com/ads/library
●Search the client's top competitor → see every ad they are running
●Note: what images they use, what headline style, what offer they promote
●Ads running 30+ days = proven winners — model your creative on those
2
🎨
Build the Ad Creative
Canva → 1080×1080 or 1080×1920 Story size
●Headline: 5 words max — lead with the result or the pain point
●Use real photos of the client's work — stock photos convert poorly for local ads
●Write body copy in ChatGPT using PAS → keep under 150 words
3
📘
Set Up the Campaign
Meta Ads Manager — business.facebook.com
●Campaign: Objective = Leads · Name it: BusinessName_Goal_Month_Year
●Ad Set: Location = 10–15 mile radius · Age/gender matching the customer · Budget $10–$15/day
●Ad: Upload creative → paste headline + body copy → CTA: "Get Quote" or "Learn More"
🚀 Click Publish — ad goes live within 24 hrs
4
⏳
Let It Run 3–5 Days
Do not touch it — the algorithm needs data
●Meta needs 50+ events to exit the learning phase — resist changing anything early
●If zero leads after Day 3 with normal spend, pause and swap the creative only
5
📊
Weekly Optimization
Every Monday — 15 minutes
●Check: Cost Per Lead (CPL) — target varies by industry ($10–$40 is typical for local)
●CPL too high? Pause the ad, create a new creative, restart — keep audience the same
●Winning ad? Duplicate the ad set, raise budget 20% — never more than 20% at once
6
📹
Send Monthly Report
Loom — record a 3-min screen walkthrough
●Open Loom → Screen + Camera → record yourself walking through Ads Manager
●Say: "You got 18 leads. Each cost $14. If you close 3 at $400 each = $1,200 from $252 spent."
●Paste Loom link in email → this one step is your best retention tool
📩 Output: Client sees ROI — retains every time

CONTENT CREATION TOOLS
🎨
Canva
Free graphic design tool
Open Canva →
How to use it in the course:
1. Go to canva.com and click Sign Up Free — use your Google account.
2. Click Create a design → search Instagram Post for social media graphics.
3. To make an ad creative: choose a template, click any text to edit it, change colors by clicking the shape, add your client's logo by clicking Uploads.
4. For Reels covers and Story graphics: search Instagram Story in templates.
5. When done: click Share → Download → choose PNG for graphics, MP4 for video.
6. Save client brand colors and logos: click Brand Kit in the left sidebar to store them permanently.
🎬
CapCut
Free Reels and short video editor
Open CapCut →
How to use it in the course:
1. Download CapCut from the App Store (phone) or capcut.com (computer).
2. Tap New Project → select your video from your camera roll.
3. Trim dead air: tap the clip on the timeline, drag the edges to cut out any pauses.
4. Add text hook: tap Text → Add Text → type your hook line → place it at the very beginning of the video.
5. Add captions automatically: tap Text → Auto Captions → select language → Generate. This puts your spoken words on screen.
6. Add music: tap Audio → Sounds → search for trending audio or pick from the free library.
7. Export: tap the arrow icon top right → choose 1080p → Save to Camera Roll.
🤖
ChatGPT
AI writing assistant for captions and copy
Open ChatGPT →
How to use it in the course:
1. Go to chat.openai.com → Sign up free → use Google account.
2. To write captions, use this exact prompt: Write 5 Instagram captions for a [business type] called [name] in [city]. The target customer is [describe them]. Tone should be [warm/funny/professional]. Each caption must end with a question or call to action. Include 8 relevant hashtags at the end of each.
3. To write ad copy using PAS: Write a Facebook ad for a [business type] using the Problem-Agitate-Solution formula. Keep it under 150 words. End with a strong call to action.
4. Always read the output out loud — if it sounds robotic, tell ChatGPT: Rewrite this to sound more human and conversational.
5. Save your best prompts in a Google Doc called My ChatGPT Prompts so you never have to rewrite them.
SCHEDULING & ANALYTICS TOOLS
📅
Buffer
Free social media scheduling tool
Open Buffer →
How to use it in the course:
1. Go to buffer.com → Start for free → connect your client's Instagram and Facebook page.
2. To connect a client account: click Connect a Channel → choose Instagram → log into their account. They must give you admin access first.
3. To schedule a post: click Create Post → upload image or video → paste the caption → add hashtags → click Schedule → pick date and time.
4. Best posting times for most local businesses: Tuesday–Friday between 9–11am or 7–9pm local time.
5. To see analytics: click Analytics in the left sidebar → choose the account → see reach, engagement, and best-performing posts.
6. Each week, look at which post got the most reach — make more content like that post the following week.
📊
Google Analytics
Free website traffic tracker
Open Analytics →
How to use it in the course:
1. Go to analytics.google.com → sign in with Google → click Start measuring.
2. Enter your client's website URL to create a property — Google gives you a tracking code.
3. Install the tracking code on the client's website: if they use WordPress, use the Site Kit by Google plugin. If Squarespace or Wix, paste the code in the header settings.
4. Key metrics to track every month: Users (how many people visited), Sessions (total visits), Traffic Source (where visitors came from — your ads should show up here).
5. Include website traffic in your monthly report to show how your ads drive people to the site.
ADVERTISING PLATFORMS
📘
Meta Business Suite
Facebook & Instagram ads manager
Open Meta →
How to use it in the course:
1. Go to business.facebook.com → Create Account → enter your agency name.
2. To add a client: click Business Settings → Accounts → Pages → Add → Request Access to a Page → enter their Facebook Page name. Client approves from their end.
3. To run an ad: go to Ads Manager → click green Create button → choose Leads as the objective for most local businesses.
4. Campaign setup: Name it as BusinessName_Goal_Month_Year (example: IronDenGym_Leads_Jan2026) → click Next.
5. Ad Set setup: set location to 10–15 mile radius around the business → set age and gender → set daily budget ($10–$15 to start) → click Next.
6. Ad setup: upload your Canva graphic → write headline (under 7 words) → write body copy using PAS → choose Learn More or Get Quote as CTA → click Publish.
7. Check results every Monday: look at Cost Per Result — if above your target CPL, pause and create new creative.
🔵
Facebook Ad Library
Spy on any business's active ads — free
Open Ad Library →
How to use it in the course:
1. Go to facebook.com/ads/library — no login required.
2. Select country → All Ads → search any brand or business name.
3. See every ad they are currently running — images, videos, copy, and how long they have been running it.
4. Use this to research prospects before a discovery call: if they have no active ads, that is your opening — tell them their competitors are running ads and they are not.
5. Use this to study what is working in any industry — the longer an ad has been running, the better it performs (they would not keep paying for a bad ad).
🔴
Google Local Service Ads
Pay-per-call ads for service businesses
Open Google LSA →
How to use it in the course:
1. Go to ads.google.com/local-services-ads → click Get Started.
2. Enter the business type and zip code — Google will tell you if LSAs are available for that category.
3. The business must pass a background check and license verification — Google handles this automatically, takes 1–2 weeks.
4. Once approved: fill in service area, business hours, and job types the business handles.
5. Set a weekly budget — Google only charges when a real customer calls or messages through the ad.
6. Best industries for LSA: plumbers, electricians, HVAC, locksmiths, carpet cleaners, house cleaners, roofers, pest control.
7. In your monthly report: show client number of calls received and average cost per call.
🎵
TikTok for Business
TikTok ads manager
Open TikTok Ads →
How to use it in the course:
1. Go to ads.tiktok.com → click Create Now → sign up with email.
2. Choose Traffic or Lead Generation as your campaign objective.
3. TikTok key rule: the ad MUST look like a normal TikTok — raw, authentic, not a polished commercial. If it looks like an ad, people skip it instantly.
4. To run a Spark Ad (boosting an existing organic post): go to Ad Groups → Ad Format → choose Spark Ad → connect the client's TikTok account → select which post to boost.
5. Target broadly and let TikTok's algorithm find the right audience — unlike Facebook, you do not need to micro-target.
6. Budget: start at $20–$30 per day minimum for TikTok ads to gather enough data.

Wave
Free invoicing and payments
Open Wave →
How to use it in the course:
1. Go to wave.com → Create an account → enter your agency name and address.
2. Add your logo: go to Settings → Business Profile → upload logo.
3. To create an invoice: click Sales → Invoices → Create Invoice → add client name → add line item: Social Media Management — Monthly Retainer → add your monthly fee → Save.

6. Send every invoice on the 1st of the month — collect payment before doing that month's work.
